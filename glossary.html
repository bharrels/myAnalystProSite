myAnalyst features

Branded terms

The signature features you'll see across the platform, defined.

Store Health score
A single 0 to 100 score and A to F grade for every location, built from five weighted pillars and ranked against the rest of your chain over a rolling 91-day window. Dozens of metrics become one number. See how scoring works.
Growth pillar (25%)
The largest Store Health pillar, weighted 25%, measuring how a store's sales trend against its own recent history. Year-over-year momentum lifts it; decline pulls it down.
Goal pillar (25%)
A 25%-weighted Store Health pillar measuring how well a store hits its budget and targets. It rewards stores pacing ahead of plan and penalizes those behind.
Loyalty pillar (20%)
A 20%-weighted Store Health pillar reflecting retention and repeat behavior, how well a store keeps guests coming back rather than relying on new traffic.
Standing pillar (15%)
A 15%-weighted Store Health pillar ranking each store against the chain on percentiles, so top performers and laggards surface against their peers.
Operations pillar (15%)
A 15%-weighted Store Health pillar covering operational discipline, items like labor control and data-capture cleanliness that show how tightly a store is run.
LiveStats
myAnalyst's real-time, store-by-store grid of net sales, cars, full service, labor hours and dollars, average ticket, and revenue per hour, refreshed in near real-time with color-coded year-over-year deltas. Group by Market, Regional Manager, or District Manager, and drill into any store. See it on the platform.
Pacing & Forecast
A month-to-date pace versus budget paired with linear and shape-adjusted projections and confidence bands. Each location reads as ahead, on track, or behind, with the exact dollars still needed to reach goal. Explore the platform.
Signals
myAnalyst's SMS and MMS alert add-on that texts the right manager the moment a metric crosses a threshold, no app to open. Rules set by metric, location, and recipient, with escalation routing and daily summaries. See Signals and other add-ons.
KPIs & analytics

Industry & analytics terms

Metrics used across auto-service and every multi-location industry myAnalyst serves.

ARO (Average Repair Order)
The average dollar amount of a single repair order, total service revenue divided by the number of orders. A core profitability metric for auto-service operators, since small gains compound across every car.
Average Ticket
The average revenue per transaction, net sales divided by the number of orders or cars. Operators watch it to gauge upsell and service-mix performance.
Car Count
The number of vehicles serviced in a period at a location. The primary volume measure for quick-lube, tire, and repair shops, and a key input to revenue per hour and labor planning.
Labor %
Labor cost as a percentage of net sales. The central efficiency ratio for keeping payroll in line with revenue, and a recurring driver in the Operations pillar of Store Health.
Revenue per Hour
Net sales divided by labor hours worked, showing how much revenue each staffed hour produces. A single productivity figure that surfaces in LiveStats.
Repeat Rate
The share of guests who return within a defined window. Higher repeat rate signals stronger loyalty and leads long-term revenue.
Coupon ROI
The return on a coupon or discount, the incremental revenue it drives relative to its cost. Shows which promotions pay for themselves rather than discounting customers who would have visited anyway.
Bay Productivity
How much work each service bay completes in a period, such as cars or revenue per bay. Shows whether a shop's capacity is fully used or sitting idle.
Technician Efficiency
How productively technicians convert their hours into completed, billable work. Helps balance staffing against demand and spot training or scheduling gaps.
RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)
A segmentation method grouping customers by how recently they visited, how often they visit, and how much they spend. myAnalyst's Retention module builds RFM segments so you can prioritize the guests most worth winning back.
CLV (Customer Lifetime Value)
The total revenue a customer is expected to generate over the life of the relationship. Helps weigh how much to invest in acquiring and retaining each guest.
VIN-level retention
Tracking each vehicle's service lifecycle by its Vehicle Identification Number, classifying it as on-track, due-soon, overdue, or lapsed. Lets operators export precise due-for-service lists and quantify the revenue at risk.
Household-level retention
Retention measured across a whole household rather than a single vehicle or person, using identity resolution to link related customers. A truer picture of loyalty when one household services several vehicles.
Capture Rate
The share of potential traffic or eligible vehicles a location converts into paying customers. A defining metric for car-wash and other high-traffic sites with large passing demand.
Membership Mix
The proportion of revenue or volume from recurring memberships versus one-time purchases. For car-wash unlimited plans it is a leading retention signal, since members visit more and churn less.
Chemical Cost per Car
The cost of soaps, waxes, and other chemicals consumed per vehicle washed. A core margin control for car-wash operators, where small per-car savings scale across high volumes.
RevPAR (Revenue per Available Room)
A hospitality metric combining occupancy and average daily rate to measure how well available rooms are monetized, capturing both how full a property is and how much each room earns.
ADR (Average Daily Rate)
The average revenue earned per occupied room per night. ADR isolates pricing performance, separate from how many rooms are filled.
Occupancy
The percentage of available rooms that are occupied over a period. Read alongside ADR, it explains the two levers behind RevPAR.
Daypart
A defined slice of the operating day, such as breakfast, lunch, or dinner, analyzed separately. Helps QSR and fast-food operators match staffing and throughput to demand across the day.
Food Cost %
The cost of ingredients as a percentage of food sales. The central margin control for QSR and fast-food operators, where a point of food cost moves real money.
Throughput
The number of transactions or guests a location serves in a period. Often paired with drive-thru times to measure speed of service and capacity to handle volume.
Basket Size
The average number of items or dollars per transaction in retail and convenience stores. A larger basket means each visit is worth more, regardless of customer count.
Shrink
Inventory loss from theft, error, or waste, expressed as a percentage of sales. It is a core loss-prevention metric in retail and convenience operations.
Drive-time Isochrone
A map boundary enclosing every point reachable within a set travel time from a location. The Geospatial Map uses isochrones to define realistic trade areas based on how far customers will drive.
Choropleth
A map that shades geographic areas by the value of a metric, making spatial patterns easy to compare. myAnalyst uses choropleths for measures like gross sales and guest count across regions.
Trade Area
The geographic region a location draws most of its customers from, drawn as a polygon or drive-time boundary. Underpins site selection, demand analysis, and guest-origin mapping.
Void
A canceled or reversed transaction at the point of sale. myAnalyst's Auditing & Compliance module monitors voids as both a loss-prevention and a data-quality signal.
Over/Short
The difference between expected and counted cash at close, over means more cash than recorded, short means less. It is a key cash-control check tracked in Auditing & Compliance.
