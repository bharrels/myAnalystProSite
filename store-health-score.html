Store Health

What is a
Store Health score?

A single 0 to 100 rating and A to F grade for every location, built from five weighted pillars and ranked against your own chain, so you know which store needs you before you even look.

Book a demo See it in the platform

A Store Health score is a 0 to 100 rating and A to F grade myAnalyst assigns to every location, built from five weighted pillars and ranked against the operator's own chain. Instead of scanning dozens of separate reports for each store, an operator reads one number and one letter, then drills into the pillars behind it. Because the score is chain-relative, it answers the question most multi-unit leaders actually have: of my locations, which ones are healthy, which need watching, and which are at risk right now?

The five pillars

Every Store Health score is a weighted blend of five pillars. Each pillar is itself scored 0 to 100, then combined using the weights below into the store's overall number. The two largest pillars, Growth and Goal, together make up half the score, because sustained growth and hitting budget are the clearest signs of a healthy location.

  • Growth · 25%, Is the store growing? This pillar looks at how the location is trending versus its own past, rewarding stores that are moving in the right direction over time.
  • Goal · 25%, Is the store hitting its budget and goals? This pillar reflects performance against the targets the operator has set for that location.
  • Loyalty · 20%, Are guests coming back? This pillar captures retention and repeat behavior, the foundation of durable revenue.
  • Standing · 15%, Where does the store stand relative to the rest of the chain? This pillar reflects the location's competitive position among its peers.
  • Operations · 15%, Is the store running clean? This pillar reflects operational discipline and execution day to day.

How it's calculated

Each of the five pillars is scored on a chain-relative percentile: myAnalyst ranks every location against the rest of your chain and asks where this store falls. A pillar score of 85 means the store is near the top of your chain on that pillar; a 58 means it is in the lower-middle. The percentiles are computed over a rolling 91-day window, so the score reflects roughly the last quarter of activity rather than a single day or a stale snapshot.

The five pillar scores are then combined using their weights, Growth 25%, Goal 25%, Loyalty 20%, Standing 15%, Operations 15%, into a single number from 0 to 100. That number is finally mapped to a familiar A to F grade, so a store's status reads at a glance whether you prefer the number or the letter. Because everything is ranked inside your own chain, the score grades each location fairly against its true peers, your other stores, rather than against an outside benchmark.

Status and trends

Beyond the number and grade, each store carries a plain-language status, healthy, watch, or at-risk, so leaders can triage the portfolio in seconds. To show direction as well as position, myAnalyst plots a 12-week trend for every store's score, and automatically surfaces movers: the locations whose Store Health is rising or falling fastest. A store that is technically "watch" but climbing six points in a week tells a very different story than one quietly sliding, and the trend makes that distinction obvious.

A worked example

Take Store 0142 in the card above. Its five pillar scores are:

  • Growth, 72. Trending up, comfortably in the upper half of the chain.
  • Goal, 64. Behind on budget relative to peers; one of the two pillars dragging the score down.
  • Loyalty, 85. Strong repeat behavior, the store's best pillar.
  • Standing, 81. Holds a strong competitive position in the chain.
  • Operations, 58. The lowest pillar; the clearest opportunity to lift the grade.

Weighted together, those pillars roll up to 78 out of 100, a B, with a status of "watch" that is improving (up six points this week). The takeaway is immediate: this is a fundamentally strong store on Loyalty and Standing, and the fastest way to move it from a B toward an A is to address Operations and close the Goal gap. That is the whole point of the score, it does not just rate the store, it points at what to fix. Store Health · FAQ

Common questions about the score

How is the Store Health score weighted?

The score is a weighted blend of five pillars: Growth (25%), Goal (25%), Loyalty (20%), Standing (15%), and Operations (15%). Each pillar is scored 0 to 100 on a chain-relative percentile, then combined into a single 0 to 100 number and mapped to an A to F grade.

Why is my store a B?

A B grade means the store sits in the upper-middle of your chain overall, but one or more pillars are pulling the blended score down. Because pillars are ranked against your own chain, a B reflects where the store stands relative to your other locations, open the store to see which pillar (for example Goal or Operations) is the lowest and is holding the grade back.

How often does the Store Health score update?

Store Health is built on the same data that powers the rest of myAnalyst, which refreshes every 5 minutes, and the pillars are recalculated on a rolling 91-day window. A 12-week trend line and automatic movers show how each store's score is changing over time.

Is the Store Health score comparable across chains?

No. The score is deliberately chain-relative, every pillar is a percentile against your own locations, not an external benchmark. That makes it a fair ranking inside one operator's portfolio, but a 78 at one chain is not directly comparable to a 78 at a different chain.
