Operations

The four numbers that decide a home-services day

Average job value, close rate, technician utilization, and jobs per day, scored per crew and per technician, refreshed every five minutes from your field-service software.

Average Job Value report

Average billed revenue per completed job, running at roughly $612 across the portfolio, broken out by crew, technician, service line, and territory, with year-over-year deltas and the share of jobs that included an add-on or upsell. See which technicians lift ticket and which leave money on the truck.

Close Rate & Sold-Job report

Sold jobs as a percentage of estimates and dispatched calls, tracking near a 47% close rate chain-wide, by technician, lead source, and job type. Surfaces who quotes well but rarely closes, and where unsold estimates are leaking demand you already paid to generate.

Technician Utilization & Billable-Hours report

Billable hours as a share of available hours per technician and crew, plus drive time, on-site time, and idle gaps between calls. Pinpoints under-loaded trucks and over-booked techs so dispatch can rebalance the board before revenue per day slips.

Jobs-per-Day & Tech Revenue-per-Day report

Completed jobs and revenue per technician per day, averaging about $1,840 per tech per day, with first-job and last-job timestamps, jobs-per-truck trends, and rankings. The clearest read on whether the schedule is full and crews are productive.

LiveStats

Every crew, side by side, updating while you watch

A real-time grid of crews and territories, revenue, jobs, average job value, close rate, and year-over-year movement. Outliers jump out in green and red so a manager can open the one crew that needs coaching today.

Sales & Marketing

Know which channels and ZIP codes actually pay

Tie booked and sold revenue back to the campaign, lead source, and neighborhood that produced it, so spend follows the jobs that close.

Marketing ROI by Channel report

Revenue produced per channel divided by spend, search, LSAs, direct mail, yard signs, referrals, and franchisor co-op, with cost per booked job, cost per sold job, and ROI ranked best to worst. See which channels return real revenue at your $612 average job value, not just cheap leads.

Lead Source & Booking-Conversion report

How many leads each source generates, the share that becomes a booked appointment, and the share that becomes a sold job at the 47% close rate. Exposes sources that flood the phones but never convert, and the quiet referral channels that close every time.

Guest-Origin & Service-Area Map

A geospatial heatmap of where your jobs actually come from, plotted by customer address and drive-time trade area, overlaid with revenue, close rate, and crew coverage. Find the neighborhoods worth more marketing and the territory edges where drive time is eating the day.

Retention & Membership

Your installed base is the cheapest revenue you have

Track every household and service agreement, who's loyal, who's due, who's slipping away, with the at-risk revenue attached.

Repeat-Customer & Membership Retention report

Repeat-job rate, active service-agreement and membership counts, renewal rate, and churn, by territory and crew. Tracks first-time vs returning revenue mix and customer lifetime value (CLV) so you can see whether the base is growing or quietly leaking.

Due-for-Service & At-Risk Revenue report

An exportable list of households due or overdue for a recurring service, tune-ups, filter changes, inspections, seasonal maintenance, each tagged with expected revenue and the total at-risk dollars per territory. Hand it to a crew or a campaign before the customer calls a competitor.

RFM Customer Segmentation report

Recency, Frequency, and Monetary scoring that sorts your customer base into segments, champions, loyal, at-risk, and lapsed, with the household count and revenue in each. Target win-back offers at high-value lapsed customers instead of blasting the whole list.

Finance & Reconciliation

Where the money came in, and where it left the truck

Means of payment, financing, and cash movement, reconciled per crew and territory so the deposit always ties out.

Means of Payment & Financing report

Revenue split by payment method, card, cash, check, ACH, and consumer financing, with financing approval rate and average financed ticket by crew. See how much of your $612 average job is carried on financing and where cash handling concentrates.

Paid In / Paid Out & Cash Reconciliation report

Every paid-in and paid-out event, credit memo, and over/short variance by crew, technician, and day. Flags cash that doesn't reconcile against jobs completed so loss prevention catches it the same day instead of at month-end close.

Cross-cutting · Store Health & Pacing

One 0 to 100 grade per crew and territory

Every crew and territory gets a single Store Health score built from five weighted pillars, Growth 25%, Goal 25%, Loyalty 20%, Standing 15%, Operations 15%, and ranked against the rest of your operation. Pacing & Forecast then projects month-to-date revenue against budget so you know who's ahead, on track, or behind before the month closes.

Signals

FAQ

Home-services reporting questions

Which home-services reports does myAnalyst include?

myAnalyst ships a full library grouped by use case: Operations (average job value, close rate, technician utilization, jobs per day and tech revenue per day), Sales & Marketing (marketing ROI by channel, lead source and booking conversion, and a guest-origin service-area map), Retention (repeat-customer and membership tracking, due-for-service lists, RFM segments and at-risk revenue), Finance (means of payment and financing, paid in/out and cash reconciliation), plus cross-cutting Store Health, Pacing & Forecast, and SMS Signals. Every report reads from your POS or field-service software read-only and refreshes every five minutes.

Can I see reports by crew, technician, and territory?

Yes. Home-services operators run many small profit centers, individual crews, trucks, technicians, and territories, rather than fixed storefronts. myAnalyst treats each crew or territory as a location, so LiveStats, Store Health, Pacing, rankings, and Signals all roll up and drill down by crew, technician, branch, or service area. You can group a region under a manager and still open a single technician's billable hours and close rate.

How does myAnalyst measure marketing ROI for home services?

The Marketing ROI report ties booked and sold revenue back to the lead source and campaign that produced each job, then divides revenue by spend to show ROI per channel. Combined with the Lead Source and booking-conversion report and the guest-origin map, you can see which channels and ZIP codes generate jobs that actually close at your average job value, not just raw lead volume.

Does myAnalyst replace my field-service or scheduling software?

No. myAnalyst reads your existing system read-only and turns it into live cross-crew reporting, scoring, pacing, and alerts. You keep dispatching, invoicing, and scheduling where you do today; myAnalyst sits on top to give owners and managers one real-time picture across every crew and territory, refreshed every five minutes.
