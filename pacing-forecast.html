Pacing · FAQ

Common questions about pacing

How does the projection work?

Two ways. A linear projection extends month-to-date sales evenly across the remaining days. A shape-adjusted projection weights the forecast by your store's own same-day-of-month curve, so weekend-heavy or end-of-month-heavy months are not under- or over-counted early.

When can I trust the forecast?

The shape-adjusted projection stabilizes within the first handful of business days, which is why operators say they know by the 5th. A confidence band widens or narrows with how much of the month has been observed.

What do ahead, on-track, and behind mean?

Each store's projected end-of-month total is compared to its budget goal. Roughly 101% or more of goal reads ahead, about 97 to 101% reads on-track, and under 97% reads behind, with the exact dollars to goal shown alongside.

Can I pace against last year instead of budget?

Yes. Compare the projection to a budget goal or to the same period last year, per store and for the whole chain, so you can see pace against plan and pace against history side by side.
